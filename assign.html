K-Pop Groups

Super Junior

Super Junior also known as SuJu, is a South Korean boy group that debuted in 2005. Originally, they consisted of thirteen members. Currently, there are only nine active members remaining. Each member is taking part in their own activities. Some are TV Show hosts, many have their own YouTube Channel, casted in musicals for their vocal abilities, and subgroups are preparing for comebacks. Their fandom name is E.L.F, which stands for Ever Lasting Friends. SuJu are recognized globally. By the end of May, they will have a paid online concert, Super Junior Beyond LIVE. It will be on May 31st, Korea Time at 3pm.

JYJ

JYJ are names of a three-member boy group, Jaejoong, Yoochun, and Junsu. This group was formed in 2010, when they left a five-member group, TVXQ. Yoochun departed from the group in April 2019, and has retired from the industry. The remaining two members both are doing solo activities. Junsu is mainly casted as the main character in musicals. Jaejoong is frequently invited on Talk Shows in Japan. He also recently released a new album in January 2020, and he has acted in Korean Dramas in the past too.

IU aka Lee Ji-Eun

Debuted at only fifteen years old, IU was Korea's "Little Sister". IU is a singer-songwriter soloist. Her vocals are of a perfect pitch. She's a natural-born singer. She's also an actress and has starred in many Korean Dramas. IU is an Ambassador for South Korea's National Police Agency in their anti-bullying campaigns in schools. She recently did a collaboration with BTS's Suga who also produced her single, "Eight".
